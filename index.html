Group Study

Group work or cooperative learning is a method of instruction that brings students together to work in groups. Employers value a person's ability to work cooperatively. Indeed, studies show that they value it more highly than the ability to work independently. This is because, in most contemporary workplaces, people work in teams, which are often cross-disciplinary and quite diverse (DETYA, 2000).

The value to students of cooperative learning has long been well recognised.
